Payment Technology Platform

Building a financial platform that gives small businesses and individuals access to more cash flow and opportunities to maximize credit card benefits

Reap, 2019-2020

The Problem

Reap is a growing payment technology platform that aims to help SMEs (small to medium-sized enterprises) unlock cash flow to accelerate their success. Reap can help simplify business payments, whether cross-border or local, and provide instant credit to pay rents, salaries, invoices, taxes, vendors, and more, while using their preferred credit card to collect rewards like cash back and Air Miles. And for individuals, Reap allows for flexible payments for tuition fees and utilities, rather than standard debit or bank transfers.

As a startup, Reap is continually aiming to attract and gain the trust of new users. The platform was originally built for functionality over visual design, and was outdated in terms of usability, responsiveness, branding, and industry standards, resulting in poor impressions, user frustrations, and drop-offs.

Functional Testing for QA

With the product and engineering teams, I provided quality assurance tests to ensure our product met usability standards without errors or difficulties.

The Final Designs

Improving branding and accessibility

In redesigning the Reap platform, my goals included creating a brand guideline/design system, improving usability, meeting accessibility standards, and creating a responsive site. I researched Reap’s competitors and other business within the industry to learn what our users were expecting from us, and conducted qualitative research to find what pains and frustrations our users were experiencing.

Building trust through communication

Based on our analytical data, the majority of users who signed up for a Reap account would not go through the process of making a payment (collecting payments was in development at the time). From this, we inferred that our users wanted to know how Reap worked, stopping just before entering their personal financial information. This lead to the development of the Reap Guide, a comprehensive onboarding guide that details how Reap can be used, and explains the security measures we take to keep user information safe.

Adopting best practices of form design

To better organize the multi-screen payment form, I utilized best practices including simplifying with conditional fields (different fields are required for different beneficiaries types), in-line validation, field focus (while scrolling, i.e. keyboard doesn’t cover input field), and logically grouped fields.

My Takeaways

I think the form fields design was done relatively well at the time, but now that I reflect on it, there are still details that could have been added on different pages to remove memory burdens, and provide important information earlier. It was my first time building a design system, so it was a bit simpler as I was learning how to build a proper one, but there was a lot more I could have done, and I didn’t get a chance to build it up as we were building out more product offerings. In addition to the design system, I also would want to update the UI to create better hierarchy by adding more depth and elevation to the designs.
